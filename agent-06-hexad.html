I. The First Perfect Number

A perfect number equals the sum of its proper divisors (divisors excluding the number itself). 6 is the first and smallest perfect number.

Divisors of 6:
$$\text{Divisors}(6) = \{1, 2, 3, 6\}$$ $$\text{Proper divisors}(6) = \{1, 2, 3\}$$
Perfect Number Condition:
$$\sigma(n) = 2n \quad \text{where } \sigma(n) \text{ is sum of all divisors}$$ $$\sigma(6) = 1 + 2 + 3 + 6 = 12 = 2 \times 6 \checkmark$$
Equivalently:
$$s(n) = n \quad \text{where } s(n) \text{ is sum of proper divisors}$$ $$s(6) = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 \checkmark$$

The Euclid-Euler Theorem

Euclid discovered that perfect numbers have the form:

$$P_n = 2^{p-1}(2^p - 1) \quad \text{where } 2^p - 1 \text{ is prime (Mersenne prime)}$$ $$P_1 = 2^{2-1}(2^2 - 1) = 2 \times 3 = 6$$ $$P_2 = 2^{3-1}(2^3 - 1) = 4 \times 7 = 28$$ $$P_3 = 2^{5-1}(2^5 - 1) = 16 \times 31 = 496$$

Properties of Perfect Numbers

  • All known perfect numbers are even (unknown if odd perfect numbers exist)
  • All even perfect numbers end in 6 or 28 in base 10
  • Extremely rare: Only 51 perfect numbers known as of 2024
  • Linked to Mersenne primes: Finding perfect numbers = finding Mersenne primes
  • Self-complementary: 6 = 1×2×3 and 6 = 1+2+3
