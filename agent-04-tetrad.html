Simplicial Complexes and Homology

Simplicial complexes are built from simplices (vertices, edges, triangles, tetrahedra, etc.) glued together according to rules:

  • Every face of a simplex in the complex is also in the complex
  • The intersection of any two simplices is a face of both (or empty)

Why Simplices?

  • Minimal: n-simplex is minimal n-dimensional object with volume
  • Rigid: A simplex cannot be deformed (all edges determine all angles)
  • Universal: Any manifold can be triangulated into simplices
  • Computational: Simplicial complexes enable algebraic topology (homology groups)

Structural Stability: Why Tetrahedra?

In 3D, the tetrahedron is the ONLY rigid polyhedron:

  • Triangle (2D): Rigid - 3 edges determine the shape
  • Tetrahedron (3D): Rigid - 6 edges determine 4 vertices
  • Cube (3D): NOT rigid - can flex without changing edge lengths
  • Higher polyhedra: Generally flexible unless triangulated

This is why geodesic domes, space frames, and molecular structures use tetrahedral lattices - they provide maximum stability with minimum material.
